## What are your core values?

My core values are the inner compass of Sacred Kaleidoscope Community, LifeCharter, and everything I am building with AI.

## 1. Alignment

Alignment is the foundation of everything I teach and everything I am building.

For me, alignment means living, choosing, creating, and serving from Truth rather than fear, pressure, reaction, or performance.

## 3. Truth

Truth is one of my deepest values.

Not truth as a weapon. Truth as a liberator.

I want to tell the truth about fear, identity, avoidance, responsibility, spiritual disconnection, and the stories we carry. I want my work to help people see what is real beneath the noise of circumstance, emotion, survival patterns, and old conditioning.

Truth creates trust.

I believe transformation requires responsibility, but not shame.

Sacred responsibility means I own my part, take the next aligned action, and stop outsourcing my life to fear, circumstance, or confusion. It is the practice of saying, "This may not all be my fault, but my next faithful step is still mine to choose."

Inner freedom is the heartbeat of my work.

I believe freedom is not only circumstantial. It is spiritual, mental, emotional, and practical. A person can be externally constrained and still begin to experience internal liberation. A person can also have every outward option and still be trapped by fear, resentment, shame, or old identity patterns.

LifeCharter, Conversations of Consequence, Becoming Uncaged, and Sacred Kaleidoscope all point back to this: we are not here to be managed by fear. We are here to remember Truth and live from it.

publishing social media posts

launching campaigns

changing automations

modifying CRM workflows

changing prices or offers

making promises to clients or leads

spending money

signing up for paid tools

canceling subscriptions

deleting files, contacts, automations, or assets

using private stories publicly

sharing personal or client information

making decisions that affect Tom, my team, clients, incarcerated correspondents, or community members
