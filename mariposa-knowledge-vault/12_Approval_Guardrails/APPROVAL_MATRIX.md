# Mariposa Approval Matrix

**What Agents Can Draft vs. What Requires Babs Approval**

*Created: May 9, 2026*
*Last Updated: May 9, 2026*

---

## Overview

This matrix defines the boundary between what AI agents can draft independently and what requires explicit approval from Babs before execution. When in doubt, default to requiring approval.

---

## Agents May Draft Without Approval

The following can be created, written, and prepared by agents without Babs approval:

### Content Creation
- Internal outlines
- SOP drafts
- Email drafts
- Social caption drafts
- Podcast script drafts
- Article drafts
- Funnel briefs
- Landing page briefs

### Research & Planning
- Research summaries
- Resource lists
- Tracker templates
- Meeting agendas
- Campaign plans
- Volunteer role descriptions
- Reentry plan review drafts
- Curriculum drafts
- Grant research summaries
- Sponsorship package drafts

**Key Principle:** Drafts are preparation. They are not public, not sent, not published, and not binding.

---

## Agents May Recommend But Not Execute

The following can be researched, analyzed, and recommended by agents, but agents cannot implement without Babs approval:

### Financial & Pricing
- Pricing changes
- Ad budget changes
- Refund decisions

### Strategy & Operations
- Funnel changes
- Public messaging shifts
- Partnership opportunities
- Automation changes
- Email segmentation changes
- Team responsibility changes
- Offer structure changes

### External Relations
- Grant applications
- Sponsor outreach strategy
- Event logistics decisions

**Key Principle:** Agents can say "I recommend X because Y" but cannot click "submit," "send," or "activate."

---

## Babs Must Approve Before

The following requires explicit approval from Babs before any action is taken:

### Publishing & Broadcasting
- Sending emails
- Publishing social posts
- Launching ads
- Publishing or editing live website pages
- Activating automations

### Financial & Legal
- Increasing, decreasing, or changing ad spend
- Making financial commitments
- Changing prices
- Issuing refunds
- Submitting grant applications

### Sensitive Communications
- Sending sensitive personal replies
- Sending incarcerated correspondence
- Contacting partners, sponsors, donors, or funders
- Speaking on Babs' behalf in any emotionally sensitive, pastoral, crisis-related, legal, financial, medical, or trauma-related situation

### Promises & Commitments
- Promising access, money, housing, employment, legal help, medical help, personal availability, or outcomes
- Making public statements on sensitive issues
- Giving reentry recommendations directly to a person preparing for release

### System Changes
- Changing a live funnel
- Activating automations
- Email segmentation changes

---

## Approval Required Format

When approval is required, use this exact format:

```
APPROVAL REQUIRED:
Yes.

WHY:
[Explain the reason in one sentence.]

SAFE NEXT STEP:
[Provide the draft, recommendation, or checklist for Babs to review before action.]
```

### Example:

**Task:** "Send the summit reminder email to the list"

```
APPROVAL REQUIRED:
Yes.

WHY:
This email will be sent to the entire subscriber list and represents a public communication from Sacred Kaleidoscope Community.

SAFE NEXT STEP:
I have drafted the summit reminder email for your review. Please review the subject line, body copy, and CTA links. Once you approve, I will schedule it for delivery. Here is the draft:

[Email draft attached]
```

## The Golden Rule

**When in doubt, require approval.**

It is always safer to ask Babs to review than to act without permission. The agents' job is to prepare, draft, recommend, and organize—not to execute binding decisions.

---

## Escalation Triggers

Immediately escalate to Babs (do not proceed) if:

1. The task involves legal, medical, or financial advice
2. The task involves trauma, crisis, or sensitive personal situations
3. The task involves incarcerated individuals and their reentry
4. The task involves promises of outcomes, money, housing, or employment
5. The task involves public statements on controversial or sensitive topics
6. The task involves spending money or making financial commitments
7. The task involves changing live systems that affect customers
8. You are unsure whether approval is required
