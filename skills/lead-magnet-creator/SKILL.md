## Workflow

### Phase 1: Audience & Topic Research

**Step 1: Identify Target Audience**
> ⏳ "First, let's figure out who this lead magnet is for. Tell me about your ideal customer."
> ✅ "Great, I have a clear picture of your target audience now."

Define the specific demographic, their pain points, and their desired outcomes.

**Step 2: Analyze Competitor Offers**
> ⏳ "Next, I'll research what your competitors are offering to find opportunities for us."
> ✅ "I've analyzed the competitive landscape and found a great angle for your lead magnet."

Research what lead magnets competitors are offering and identify gaps or opportunities.

**Step 3: Select Lead Magnet Type**
> ⏳ "Now, let's choose the best format. Should we create a checklist, an ebook, or something else?"
> ✅ "A [checklist] is the perfect format for your audience. It gives them a quick win!"

Choose the most effective format (e.g., checklist, template, ebook, cheat sheet) based on the audience's need for instant gratification vs. deep knowledge.

**Step 4: Develop the Hook**
> ⏳ "Let's create a catchy title that grabs attention and promises a great result."
> ✅ "I've drafted a few title options for you to choose from."

Create a compelling, benefit-driven title and hook that promises a specific, achievable result.

💡 **Pro tip:** A great title clearly states the benefit the user will get. For example, instead of "Gardening Guide," try "The 5-Step Guide to Your First Abundant Vegetable Garden."

**Step 2: Draft the Copy**

> ✅ "I've added a call-to-action and other conversion elements to the draft."

Add credibility markers (author bio, testimonials), clear calls-to-action (CTAs), and natural transitions to the user's paid offer.

**Step 4: Review and Refine**
> ⏳ "I'm now editing the content for clarity, conciseness, and to match your brand's voice."

Edit for clarity, conciseness, and persuasive language. Ensure the tone matches the user's brand.

## Multi-step workflows

### Workflow 1: The "Quick-Win Checklist" Generator
1.  **Extract:** Take a long-form piece of content (e.g., a comprehensive blog post or podcast transcript) provided by the user.
2.  **Distill:** Identify the core actionable steps and distill them into a concise, step-by-step checklist.
3.  **Format:** Structure the checklist with clear checkboxes, brief explanations, and a professional layout.
4.  **Package:** Output the final checklist as a clean, branded PDF ready for immediate download.

### Workflow 2: The "Authority Ebook" Builder
1.  **Research:** Conduct deep research on a specific industry topic requested by the user.
2.  **Draft:** Write a comprehensive 5-10 page guide, including an introduction, core chapters, case studies, and a conclusion.
3.  **Design:** Format the text with professional typography, chapter headings, and placeholders for images/charts.
4.  **Monetize:** Embed strategic links and a strong final CTA directing readers to the user's paid services or consultation booking.

### Workflow 3: The "Swipe File" Assembler
1.  **Curate:** Gather examples of high-performing content (e.g., email subject lines, ad copy, social media hooks) relevant to the user's niche.
2.  **Categorize:** Organize the examples into logical categories for easy reference.
3.  **Annotate:** Add brief notes explaining *why* each example works and how the user's audience can adapt it.
4.  **Deliver:** Package the collection into a structured document or spreadsheet template.

## Guardrails
- **Focus on One Problem:** Ensure the lead magnet solves one specific problem rather than trying to cover too much ground.
- **Prioritize Instant Value:** Design the content so the user can achieve a quick win within 15 minutes of consuming it.
- **Maintain High Quality:** Never sacrifice quality for speed; a poor lead magnet damages brand trust.
- **Include Clear CTAs:** Always include a clear next step or call-to-action leading to the user's core offer.
- **Optimize for Mobile:** Ensure the final document is easily readable on mobile devices.
- **Avoid Overwhelming Text:** Use formatting (bullets, bolding, short paragraphs) to prevent walls of text.
- **Ensure Brand Consistency:** Match the tone, voice, and visual style to the user's existing brand.
- **Don't Give Everything Away:** Provide enough value to build trust, but leave the ultimate solution for the paid offer.

## Failure handling

| Error Scenario | User-Friendly Message |
| :--- | :--- |
| Low opt-in conversion rate | ❌ "It seems not many people are signing up. Let's try rewriting the headline on your landing page to make the benefit crystal clear." |
| High unsubscribe rate after download | ❌ "People are unsubscribing right after they get the download. Let's make sure the content perfectly matches the promise you made on the sign-up page." |
| Content feels generic | ❌ "The content feels a bit generic. Let's add some specific examples, case studies, or your own unique insights to make it stand out." |
| Document looks unprofessional | ❌ "The design could be more polished. I can help you reformat it with a clean, modern template to make it look more professional." |
| Leads aren't converting to sales | ❌ "Your new leads aren't turning into customers. Let's adjust the lead magnet to create a smoother transition to your paid offer." |
| File size too large | ❌ "The file is a bit large, which might make it slow to download. I can compress the images and optimize the file to make it smaller." |
| Content is too long/overwhelming | ❌ "This might be too much information for a quick freebie. How about we edit it down to the absolute essentials, or turn it into a multi-part email course?" |
| No clear next step | ❌ "There's no clear call-to-action at the end. Let's add a compelling next step to guide your new subscribers on what to do next." |
